Kuching is approximately 35 kilometers north of the state capital Kuching. You can reach it by private transport or taxi, which takes about 45 minutes and wil cost about RM26-RM32 per pax. Some visitor prefer hiring a tour bus or by shuttle van for convenience.

Kampung Santubong is known for its natural beauty, coastal scenery and cultural heritage. Key sites include Mount Santubong, a 810-meter tall mountain that popular destination for hiking and offering stunning views, and the Sarawak Cultural Village, which provides insights into the diverse cultures of Sarawak ethnic groups. The Damai Beach and opportunities to see the rare wildlife like Irrawaddy dolphin are also highlights.

Visitors can engage in various activities such as hiking and trekking in the rainforest on Mount Santubong, exploring the Sarawak Cultural Village, Swimming and beach activities like volleyball at the Damai beach, The area is also strategic for wildlife spotting such as see the rare Irrawaddy dolphins

The best time to visit is during the dry season. Which is typically runs from February to April. During this period offers the best weather with average temperatures around 29Â°C And it is best for outdoor activities and exploring the area scenary.

There are various accommodations ranging from luxury resorts to more rustic homestays. Popular options include Damai Beach Resort, Permai Rainforest Resort, and Santubong Suites Homestay. These resorts and homestay offer different amenities like swimming pools, restaurant and more upscale accommodation experience.

The legend of Puteri Santubong and Puteri Sejinjang, two princesses who were turned into mountains after a curse, is a significant part of the area's history. There are also historical sites like Sungai Jaong, This park contains carved stones along the Jaong River that provide evidence of human settlement dating back over a thousand years.
